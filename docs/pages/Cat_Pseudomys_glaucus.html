Blue-gray mouse

Pseudomys glaucus

Blamed on cats

IUCN Status: Extinct

Last Seen: Pseudomys glaucus were last seen in 1956 in between Burren and Walgett in northern New South Wales

Studies in support

Blue-gray mice were last confirmed in QLD 55 years after cats arrived (Wallach et al.202X).

Studies not in support

Blue-gray mice were last confirmed in NSW 115 years after cats arrived (Wallach et al.202X).

Is the threat claim evidence-based?

There are no studies evidencing a link between cats and the extinction of blue-gray mice. In contradiction with the claim, the two species co-occurred for over half-a-century in QLD and over a century in NSW.
