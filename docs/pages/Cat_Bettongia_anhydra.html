Desert bettong

Bettongia anhydra

Blamed on cats

IUCN status: Extinct

Last seen: Bettongia anhydra were last seen in 1933 in McEwin Hills, Lake Mackay, western Northern Territory (IUCN 2023)

IUCN claim: The Desert Bettong was probably driven to extinction because of predation by introduced Red Foxes and feral domestic cats

Studies in support

Desert bettongs were last confirmed 48 years after cats arrived (Current submission).

Studies not in support

No studies

Is the threat claim evidence-based?

There are no studies evidencing a link between cats and the extinction of desert bettongs.

Evidence linking Bettongia anhydra to cats. Last records of extirpated populations relative to earliest local records of cats. Error bars show record uncertainty range. Predator arrival records were digitized from Abbott 2008.
