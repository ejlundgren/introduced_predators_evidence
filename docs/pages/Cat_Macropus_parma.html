Macropus parma

Blamed on cats

IUCN Status: Near Threatened

EPBC Threat Rating: Moderate

IUCN Claim: N/A

Studies in support

No studies

Studies not in support

Cats were not among predators of reintroduced, predator-inexperienced, wallabies (Short et al.1992).

Is the threat claim evidence-based?

There are no studies linking cats to parma wallabies.

Systematic review of evidence for an association between Macropus parma and cats. Positive studies are in support of the hypothesis that cats contribute to the decline of Macropus parma, negative studies are not in support. Predation studies include studies documenting hunting or scavenging; baiting studies are associations between poison baiting and threatened mammal abundance where information on predator abundance is not provided; population studies are associations between threatened mammal and predator abundance.
